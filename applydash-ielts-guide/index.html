What is IELTS?

Overview

International English Language Testing System

IELTS is one of the world's most widely accepted English proficiency tests, jointly managed by the British Council, IDP: IELTS Australia, and Cambridge Assessment English. It assesses four skills — Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking — and is recognized by universities, employers, professional bodies, and governments for study, work, and migration purposes.

Test Skills

Four Modules Tested

  • Listening — 4 sections, 40 questions
  • Reading — 3 passages, 40 questions
  • Writing — 2 tasks (essay & report/letter)
  • Speaking — face-to-face or video interview

Types of IELTS Tests

IELTS Academic

Designed for candidates applying to study at universities and higher education institutions, or seeking professional registration in an English-speaking environment. The Reading and Writing modules reflect academic language used in lectures, textbooks, and reports.

  • Best for: undergraduate & postgraduate university admissions
  • Best for: professional bodies (medical, engineering, legal councils)
  • Writing Task 1: describe a graph, chart, table, or diagram

IELTS General Training

Suited for candidates migrating to English-speaking countries, or applying for work experience and training programs. The test focuses on everyday English used in social and workplace contexts.

  • Best for: skilled migration & work visas
  • Best for: secondary education enrolment
  • Writing Task 1: write a letter (formal, semi-formal, or informal)

IELTS for UKVI (UK Visas & Immigration)

Same content and format as Academic or General Training, but the results are recognized specifically for UK visa and immigration applications, including the Secure English Language Test (SELT) requirement set by the UK Home Office.

  • Available in Academic and General Training formats
  • Required for: Student visas, Skilled Worker visas, Settlement & Citizenship
  • Results delivered with a UKVI-approved test report form

Scope & Opportunities

🎓 Higher Education

Accepted by thousands of universities and colleges worldwide for undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programs.

✈️ Migration & Visas

Used for skilled migration to countries such as the UK, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand, including UKVI-specific visa categories.

💼 Employment

Recognized by employers and professional/regulatory bodies as proof of English proficiency for work environments.

🏫 School Admissions

Some secondary schools and training institutions accept IELTS scores for admission of international students.

General Requirements to Take IELTS

  • Valid passport or national identity document for registration and on test day
  • No minimum age requirement, though IELTS is generally recommended for ages 16+
  • No specific qualifications needed to register — open to all proficiency levels
  • Choice of Paper-based or Computer-delivered test formats (availability varies by test centre)
  • Results are typically valid for 2 years for most institutions and visa applications
